Harry Lime     (8 out of 10 )
This is Tarantino's most straightforward, most pensive, and least characteristic movie. Its comparative lack of blood-spattering bombast gives it almost a meandering tone in retrospect, but he skilfully subordinates a poignant, socially conscious undertone of Blaxploitation to an entertaining slow heist. It's a long film, but I could watch these characters play off each other self-interestedly all day.

Sorry to all of those people missing the blood guysers and black suit wearing gangsters, but you should broaden your horizons and judge this on its own merits for what it is, a cinematic masterpiece.
